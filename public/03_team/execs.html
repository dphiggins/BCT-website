Leadership

  • David Min
    Founder, CEO
    Bio

    David Min is a high school senior attending the Buckingham Browne & Nichols School in Cambridge, MA. Additionally during the fall of 2020, he studied at the School for Ethics and Global Leadership in Washington, DC. As Founder and Chief Executive Officer of Boston COVID-Tutoring (BCT), David heads day-to-day operations, engages in the development of ideas, objectives, special projects while overseeing BCT’s Leadership Team and communicating with tutors and families. David founded BCT during the early stages of the COVID-19 pandemic after realizing that education is a critical aspect of one’s life that can promote social mobility and develop critical thinking skills. With the pandemic having exacerbated the economic and educational inequalities in his country, David wanted to give back to his community. Inspired by his own experiences with tutoring, David hopes that students will not only receive the educational experience they may need but also the opportunity to cultivate a lasting relationship with their tutor. In his spare time, he enjoys playing the oboe, writing for his school newspaper, and spending time with his family. David hopes to truly make a positive impact in his community by providing this service to any children in need—regardless of their background.

    Dan Bi
    CTO
    Bio

    Dan Bi is a senior at Bucking Browne and Nichols High School. He is the Chief Technology Officer of BCT. He oversees all the technological challenges presented by virtual tutoring, and he manages and updates BCT's website with the help of a website development team. In addition to all the tasks above, he is also personally involved in all of BCT's on-going projects and plans for the future of BCT alongside David and Graham. Dan joined BCT at its conception and helped David kickstart the company. He wanted to aid kids who are less fortunate than him and put them on equal footing with those in financially stable households. In his free time, Dan rows for his school's crew team and enjoys working on computer science projects.

    Graham Bateman
    COO
    Bio

    Graham Bateman is a highschool Junior at Buckingham Browne & Nichols School (BB&N) in Cambridge, Massachusetts. He is the Chief Operating Officer of Boston COVID-Tutoring (BCT) and helps manage day-to-day operations. Graham also created and leads BCT’s program for Spanish-speaking unaccompanied minors. Graham’s goal is to help struggling students become confident, independent thinkers. Outside of BCT, Graham is a passionate, award-winning debater, an award-winning Model UN delegate, and an avid student of Arabic. He also helps lead the BB&N’s Community Engagement and Leadership Board, contributes regularly to his school’s political opinion magazine, and serves as a Class Representative in student government. Outside of school, Graham enjoys rowing, reading, and shooting hoops. He has an intense interest in foreign policy and enjoys having lively, intellectual conversations with friends. Graham hopes that you find working with BCT as meaningful as it has been for him.

    Christian Bateman
    Director of Devlopment
    Bio

    Christian Bateman is a high school junior at Buckingham Browne and Nichols School.  He is the BCT Director of Development and in the past he has helped support outreach efforts, led data collection in order to improve the organizational efficiency, and addressed concerns brought up by other BCT members.  As someone who has tutored in the organization since its inception, Christian has recently turned his attention to tutor training, as to ensure BCT maintains a high quality of service.  Outside of BCT, Christian is the Assistant Editor in Chief for BB&N’s political magazine, Point of View, and plays a pivotal role in the publication’s editing process and production.   To expand his knowledge on the subject, he took “Urban Education” last summer, an upper level course offered at the University of California Santa Cruz which discussed the economic and racial barriers that continue to plague education systems in urban communities.  In his spare time, Christian enjoys cooking, running, and spending quality time with his family.

    Julia Torrey
    Vice President
    Bio

    Julia Torrey is a Junior at Milton Academy. She is the Vice President of Boston COVID-Tutoring. She plays lacrosse and violin and loves to hangout with her friends in her free time. Julia began working for BCT as a tutor in August of 2020. Through her tutoring experience, Julia learned how great tutoring can be for a student's confidence. Passionate about the values of BCT, Julia decided to become more involved in the organization. In early 2021, she began to do interviews for BCT, and later on began to work on the outreach team. Now, as a member of the Leadership team, Julia manages various different projects across BCT, like the music program, the TikTok, and more. She believes that tutoring, when needed, is crucial for academic success, and hopes to better the lives and academic journeys of students through her work with Boston COVID-Tutoring.

    Tasha Roseman
    Co-Director of Outreach
    Bio

    Tasha Roseman is a high school junior attending the Buckingham Browne & Nichols School in Cambridge, MA. During the spring of 2021, she is also looking forward to studying abroad at the African Leadership Academy in South Africa.
